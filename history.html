Historical Background

The Thiruvotriyur Kanchi Kamakoti Sankara Mutt is an ancient spiritual institution closely associated with the revered Kanchi Kamakoti Peetam. It has played an important role in preserving and propagating the teachings of Jagadguru Adi Shankaracharya and the philosophy of Advaita Vedanta.

Connection to Kanchi Kamakoti Peetam

The Mutt functions as a spiritual extension of the Kanchi Kamakoti Peetam and follows its sacred traditions, rituals, and Guru Parampara. Over centuries, this Mutt has served as a centre for worship, learning, and guidance for devotees in and around Thiruvotriyur.

Adhishtanams of Great Acharyas

The Mutt is especially sacred as it houses the Adhishtanams (Jeeva Samadhis) of great Acharyas of the Kanchi Kamakoti lineage.

  • Shri Mahadevendra Saraswathy III – attained Mukti here in 1746 AD after 42 years of spiritual leadership.
  • Sri Chandrchuda III – attained Mukti in 1524 AD after 17 years of pontificate.

Spiritual Importance

Situated in the sacred town of Thiruvotriyur, near the ancient Thyagaraja Temple, the Sankara Mutt continues to inspire generations of devotees through its dedication to Dharma, devotion, and service.
